## Overview on Annual GDP Accounting - 年度GDP核算概述

### 1.1 Basic Concepts - 基本概念

<ins>Gross Domestic Product(GDP) refers to the final products at market prices produced by all resident units certain period of time.</ins> GDP is the core indicator of the <ins>national accounts</ins>, and also an important indicator to measure level of development of a country or region.

In the practice of national accounting, gross domestic product is calculated from three approaches, namely ==<ins>production approach, income approach and expenditure approach</ins>==, which reflect gross domestic product and its composition from different angles. <ins>==GDP by production approach== refers to the method from the point of view of production of measuring resident units create new value for a new accounting period,</ins> i.e. production from various sectors of the national economy during the accounting period, the total value of the product, minus the value of intermediate inputs in the production process to obtain value-added. The accounting formula as follows: `value-added = total output - intermdiate inputs`. <ins>The ==income approach== refers to the accounting method of measuring final income which obtained from the production process by the factors of production</ins> to reflect the final outcome for stand at the point of view from the production process. Under this accounting method, the sum of the value-added obtained from four parts: workers compensation, net taxes on production, depreciation of fixed assets and operating surplus. <ins>==The expenditure approach== is to measure the final destination of the products and services of the accounting period from the end use perspective,</ins> including final consumption expenditure, capital formation, total and net exports of goods and services in three parts. GDP by Expenditure Approach refers to the method of measuring the final results of production activities of a country (region) during a given period from the perspective of final uses. It includes final consumption expenditure, gross capital formation and net export of goods and services.



> 国内生产总值（GDP）指的是在一定时期内由所有常住单位以市场价格所生产的最终产品总量。GDP 是国家账户体系中的核心指标，也是衡量一个国家或地区发展水平的重要指标。

> 在国民经济核算实践中，国内生产总值（GDP）的计算采用了三种方法，即生产法、收入法和支出法，这三种方法从不同的角度反映了国内生产总值及其构成情况。通过生产法计算的 GDP 是指从生产的角度来衡量居民单位在新的会计期间创造的新价值的方法，即在会计期间内国民经济各个部门的生产活动所产生的总价值，减去生产过程中中间投入的价值，从而得出增加值。其会计公式如下：增加值 = 总产出 - 中间投入价值。收入法是指通过生产过程中生产要素所获得的最终收入来衡量的会计方法，从生产过程的角度反映最终结果。在这种会计方法下，增加值的总和来自四个部分：工人工资、生产税净额、固定资产折旧和经营盈余。支出法是从最终用途的角度来衡量会计期间内产品和服务的最终去向，包括最终消费支出、资本形成、货物和服务的总出口和净出口三部分。根据支出法计算的国内生产总值是指从最终用途的角度来衡量一个国家（地区）在特定时期内生产活动的最终成果的方法。它包括最终消费支出、总资本形成和货物及服务的净出口。

## 词汇

- national accounts - 国民经济核算: 一种统计方法，用于衡量一个国家的经济活动和产出，包括国内生产总值（GDP）、国民收入（GNI）等。
- Factors of production - 生产要素: 在经济学中， 生产要素 、 资源或投入是指生产过程中用于生产产出 （即商品和服务） 的物质或资源。各种投入的利用量决定了产出的数量，这种关系被称为生产函数 。有四种基本资源或生产要素：土地、劳动力、资本和企业家才能（或企业）。这些要素也常被称为“ ==生产资料==”或“生产服务 ”，以区别于消费者购买的商品或服务，后者通常被称为“ 消费品 ”。 [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Factors_of_production]
